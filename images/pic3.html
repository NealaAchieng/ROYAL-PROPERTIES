About Rancho Penasquitos

Twelve miles inland from Torrey Pines State Beach, the affluent community of Rancho Peasquitos sits under the little cliffs of 1,500-foot Black Mountain. Part of the first land grant from Mexico to America in 1823, this small neighborhood is now a very competitive housing market for its high ratio of parklands to pavement and its top-performing public schools. Homes in Rancho Peasquitos range from late 20th-century ranch-style homes on big lots with hidden Zen gardens to newer subdivisions of the large adobe-colored Spanish Colonials prevalent in Southern California. Private pools abound. For new buyers there are some Mission-style luxury condos near the shopping district that are less expensive than the single-family homes. This neighborhood is bordered by 2,300-acre Black Mountain Open Space Park on the northeast and the 3,700-acre Los Peasquitos Canyon County Preserve to the south. Both of these large nature preserves offer an abundance of activities for the outdoor lover, from bird-watching hikes to half-marathon trail runs. Hikers summitting the 1,500-foot peak of Black Mountain will be rewarded with 360-degree views of San Diego. Rancho Peasquitos public schools have been awarded for their technical education pathways that offer experiential learning in trades like agriculture, construction and the arts. Elementary school students have the option of learning one of nine different world languages. Local parents of skateboarding teens have the option of dropping their kids off at the Rancho Peasquitos Skate Park before shopping at the Vons market next door or getting in a quick workout at the Rancho Family YMCA. There is also an organic market and a Costco on the border of the neighborhood. For younger children, the state-of-the-art Rancho Peasquitos Library is a great place to spend an afternoon. This postmodern building has a large atrium under which toddlers can listen to local park rangers talk about the flora and fauna of the canyon or attend a variety of musical instrument lessons. There are three large, renovated community centers offering everything from badminton to basketball. Renowned East Village gastropub Knotty Barrel opened up its first western location here, as did San Diego Korean fried chicken favorite Rice Chicken. Rancho Peasquitos is also home to several great Mexican restaurants. The annual Fiesta and Fireworks Parade in June is a popular local event, where residents cut loose with carnival rides, food and live entertainment. In keeping with the outdoorsy character of Rancho Peasquitos, the local parks offer many programs for residents. Visitors to Los Peasquitos Canyon County Preserve can bravely venture out on wildlife night hikes and calm their nerves the next day during guided meditation by the waterfall of the creek winding through the park. History buffs will enjoy touring the second-oldest residence in San Diego, the Rancho Peasquitos Adobe, built right after that famous land grant. Black Mountain Open Space Park offers birding classes, and future residents will benefit from a planned expansion of the State Route 56 Bike Path which runs east to west for 10 miles and will soon connect near the beach to the North Coastal Trail.
